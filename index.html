Sales Trends (1995–2025) As part of a 5-person team in the Data Analytics Accelerator internship, we analyzed 51,000+ UK property transactions spanning 30 years across 32 postcode areas. The project explored price trends, property types, tenure comparisons, holding periods, new build premiums, and the impact of economic shocks like the 2008 financial crisis and COVID-19. My Contribution — Question 5: New Build Premium Analysis I led the analysis comparing median sale prices for new builds vs. existing homes across all 32 postcode areas and years (672 combinations). Using Excel for calculations and Tableau for geographic mapping, I found that new builds command a premium in 27 of 32 areas — ranging from +140% in Westminster (W) to −7% in Romford (RM). Prime Central London postcodes (W, SW, WC) dominated the top 3, revealing that the new build premium is highly location-dependent, not universal. ￼ Open Interactive Dashboard UK Postcode Premium Map — click to explore interactive version Key Team Findings • Flats showed the lowest median price change overall — likely due to shorter holding periods • Postcode areas W, SW, EC, and NW consistently had the highest median property costs across the decade • Short-term resales (≤2 yrs) represent only 16.2% of repeat sales; the market is structurally long-term • Median lifetime return was +121.3% over a ~19.5-year holding period • RM had the most dramatic 2008 dip with strong recovery — a pattern it repeated in 2020 • New builds command premiums in 27 of 32 areas; Central London drives the highest Tableau Viz Presentation Project Brief Dataset TBD Full Analytics Pipeline Capstone Project • Choose-your-own-topic capstone project • Final portfolio project for DAA bootcamp • Currently in progress...
